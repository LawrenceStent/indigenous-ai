Every two weeks, another Indigenous language dies. AI could help preserve them—but only if we're included.

Indigenous languages carry unique ways of understanding the world. They contain knowledge about plants, animals, weather patterns, and cultural practices developed over thousands of years. When these languages disappear, entire worldviews vanish with them.

The AI Language Gap

Dominated by Major Languages

AI language models are trained primarily on English, Chinese, Spanish, and other dominant languages. Indigenous languages are virtually absent from training datasets.

Missing Linguistic Diversity

Indigenous languages often have unique grammatical structures, sound systems, and ways of categorizing the world that could enrich AI's understanding of human language.

No Digital Footprint

Many Indigenous languages have limited digital presence, making them invisible to AI systems that rely on web-scraped data.

Solutions We Need

Community-Led Digitization

Support Indigenous communities in creating digital archives of their languages, ensuring cultural protocols are respected.

Inclusive AI Training

Develop AI models that actively include Indigenous languages and are trained with community consent and ownership.

Language Revitalization Tech

Create AI-powered tools that help communities teach and learn their ancestral languages.

Linguistic Sovereignty

Ensure Indigenous communities maintain control over their linguistic data and how it's used in AI systems.
